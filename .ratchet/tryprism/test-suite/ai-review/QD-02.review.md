## Task

## Evaluation Criteria

Evaluate all 9 descriptions as a set, then provide per-type notes where relevant.

### 1. Accuracy (weight: 30%)

For each type, verify the core attributes match established Enneagram theory:

| Type | Core Motivation | Core Fear | Core Desire |
|------|----------------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | To be good, right, and ethical | Being corrupt, evil, or defective | Integrity, improvement |
| 2 | To be loved and needed | Being unloved, unwanted | To feel loved unconditionally |
| 3 | To be valuable and successful | Being worthless, a failure | To feel valuable and worthwhile |
| 4 | To find identity and significance | Having no identity or significance | To be themselves, find meaning |
| 5 | To be capable and competent | Being helpless, incompetent | Mastery, competence |
| 6 | To have security and support | Being without support, guidance | Security and certainty |
| 7 | To be satisfied and content | Being deprived, trapped in pain | To be satisfied, fulfilled |
| 8 | To protect themselves and control | Being harmed or controlled by others | Self-reliance, strength |
| 9 | To maintain peace and harmony | Loss of connection, fragmentation | Inner stability, peace |

Check: Does each description accurately represent the type's core motivation, fear, and desire?

### 2. Tone — Empathetic and Non-Judgmental (weight: 25%)

- Are all descriptions written from a position of understanding and compassion?
- Does the description affirm the type's positive qualities before addressing challenges?
- Are the challenges described as neutral patterns (not as character flaws or moral failings)?
- Would someone of that type feel seen and understood, not judged or stereotyped?
- Are strengths genuinely celebrated, not merely listed?

Red flags to check for:
- Any description that reads as primarily negative
- Language that implies one type is better or worse than others
- Clinical/cold tone that lacks warmth
- Stereotyping (e.g., "Twos are manipulative" without nuance)

### 3. Actionability and Growth Suggestions (weight: 20%)

Each description should include:
- At least 2–3 concrete growth suggestions (not just "be more present" but specific practices or shifts in awareness)
- Reference to the type's growth direction (integration arrow: 1→7, 2→4, 3→6, 4→1, 5→8, 6→9, 7→5, 8→2, 9→3)
- A note on stress behavior or how to recognize when under stress (disintegration arrow: 1→4, 2→8, 3→9, 4→2, 5→7, 6→3, 7→1, 8→5, 9→6)
- Practical, actionable language (not vague platitudes)

### 4. Completeness — Required Sections (weight: 15%)

Each description should cover:
- [ ] Core motivation / basic desire
- [ ] Core fear
- [ ] Key strengths (3+ specific traits)
- [ ] Key challenges / growth edges
- [ ] Growth direction (what growth looks like for this type)
- [ ] Stress behavior (what regression looks like)
- [ ] Brief tagline or defining phrase

### 5. Length (weight: 10%)

- Target: 200–400 words per type in English
- Below 150 words: too thin, likely missing important content
- Above 500 words: likely padded or unfocused
