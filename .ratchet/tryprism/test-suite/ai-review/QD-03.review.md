**Dimension:** Chinese translation quality — natural, culturally adapted, not robotic

## Task

You are reviewing the Chinese (Simplified, zh-CN) localization of the TryPrism web application. This includes UI text, type descriptions, and quiz questions. Your job is to evaluate how natural, culturally appropriate, and psychologically meaningful the Chinese content is — as if reading it as a native Chinese speaker.

---

## Rubric

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **5** | Reads like native Chinese writing, culturally adapted (not literal translation), personality concepts expressed naturally in Chinese psychological vocabulary, no awkward phrasing |
| **4** | Mostly natural, one or two awkward phrasings but overall reads well; culturally appropriate |
| **3** | Understandable but clearly translated, occasional awkward phrasing that a native speaker would notice |
| **2** | Choppy or unnatural in multiple places; some culturally inappropriate phrasings |
| **1** | Robotic translation, cultural concepts lost, awkward or incorrect Chinese, mixed scripts |

**Pass threshold: 4 or above.**

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Natural Phrasing in UI Text (weight: 25%)

Evaluate the Chinese text for common button labels and UI elements:

Key items to check:
- "Start Test" → 开始测试 (natural) vs. 开始检测 (medical/technical feel — wrong register)
- "Quick Mode" → 快速模式 or 快速测试 (both fine) vs. 快捷方式 (sounds like a computer shortcut — wrong)
- "Full Mode" → 完整模式 or 完整版测试 (natural) vs. 全部模式 (unnatural)
- "Results" → 测试结果 or 结果分析 (natural) vs. 结果 alone (too bare)
- "Your primary type is" → 你的主要类型是 (too literal) vs. 你的九型人格类型是 (natural in Chinese psychology context)
- Progress indicators: "第X题 共X题" (natural) vs. "问题 X / X" (too English-structured)

Check for:
- Use of appropriate Chinese psychological/personality vocabulary
- Correct measure words (量词) where applicable
- Appropriate formality level (neither too formal/literary nor too casual/internet slang)
- No pinyin or romanized words where Chinese terms exist

### 2. Type Descriptions — Cultural Adaptation (weight: 35%)

Chinese psychology has its own vocabulary for personality and inner experience. Evaluate:

- **Enneagram-specific terms**: Are standard Chinese Enneagram terms used?
  - 九型人格 (Enneagram / nine personality types)
  - 核心恐惧 (core fear)
  - 核心渴望 (core desire) — not 核心欲望 which sounds negative
  - 成长方向 (growth direction) — not 成长箭头 (that's too literal for "arrow")
  - 压力状态 (stress state) or 压力方向 (stress direction)
  - 翼型 (wing type)

- **Register and tone**: Chinese psychological writing tends toward a slightly more formal register than English pop psychology. The descriptions should feel like they belong in a Chinese self-help / psychology book, not a translated Western blog post.

- **Cultural idioms**: Are there any Western idioms that should be replaced with Chinese equivalents?
  - "Put on a mask" → 戴上面具 (acceptable Chinese metaphor, but 压抑自我 might be more natural in context)
  - "In the driver's seat" → should be replaced with a Chinese equivalent or rephrased

- **Avoiding direct translation errors**:
  - Watch for overly literal phrasing like 他们是非常努力工作的人 (overly literal) vs. 他们勤奋上进 (natural)
  - Watch for incorrect word order imposed by English syntax

### 3. Quiz Questions — Psychological Equivalence (weight: 30%)

Sample at least 20 questions. For each, evaluate:

- Does the Chinese version capture the same psychological construct as the English?
  - Example: If English asks about "feeling responsible for others' emotions," Chinese should probe the same motivational content, not just translate words.
- Are the questions phrased in a way that works for Chinese cultural context?
  - Scenarios involving individual vs. group decisions (collectivist culture considerations)
  - Questions about "standing out" or "expressing emotions" should be calibrated for Chinese cultural norms
- Are any questions awkward or ambiguous in Chinese that are clear in English?
- Is the Likert scale labeled naturally in Chinese?
  - 非常不同意 / 不同意 / 中立 / 同意 / 非常同意 (or equivalent natural Chinese labels)

### 4. Technical Quality (weight: 10%)

- No encoding issues (no garbled characters, no ? replacements)
- No mixed scripts (no English words where Chinese should be used)
- No simplified/traditional character mixing (should be consistently Simplified Chinese)
- No pinyin substitutes for Chinese characters
- Punctuation: uses Chinese punctuation marks (，。！？「」) not English punctuation in Chinese text
